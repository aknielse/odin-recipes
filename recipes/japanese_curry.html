Japanese Curry

Description:

A sweeter touch on Indian curry. This simple curry typically uses curry cubes, with potatos, carrots, and onions. This dish is typically served over rice with chicken or pork katsu.

Ingredients:

  • 3 Onions
  • 2 Carrots
  • 2 Large golden potatoes
  • 4 Cloves of garlic
  • 4 Curry blocks/cubes
  • 1 or 2 Chicken breasts/thighs or other protein
  • 1 Carton of chicken stock (recipe needs about 2 cups)
  • 1 Tbs of soy sauce
  • 1 Tsp of ginger
  • 1 Tbs of ketchup
  • Optional: 2 Tbs of lime or lemon
  • 1 Tbs of sesame oil or neutral oil
  • Pinch of salt
  • Pinch of pepper
  • Optional: Instant-pot to cook in

Steps:

  1. Prepare the ingredients.

    1. Skin and quarter 2 golden potatoes into 2 inch chunks. Soak the potato chunks in a bowl of cold water.
    2. Cut the 3 onions in half, and then into 5 wedges each.
    3. Peel and chop the 2 carrots into 1 inch pieces. Roll the each carrot 1/4 after each cut to allow proper cooking. This is so you can increase the surface area of each piece of carrot when it cooks.
    4. Cut your protein. In this recipe we'll be using 2 chicken breasts. Cut your chick into 1 inch slices. Make sure to cut diagonally, like the carrots, to increase surface area and thorough cooking. Then season with salt and pepper.
    5. Mince 4 cloves of garlic.
    6. Peel and grate 1-2 tsp(s) of ginger and mix with the garlic.

  2. Start cooking the curry.

    1. If using an Instant-pot, set it to Saute mode (low). Once heated, add 1 tbs of oil.
    2. After adding in the oil, add in the onion wedges, along with the garlic and ginger mixture.
    3. Wait about 5 or until the onions have started to soften, and then add the carrots, potatos (without the water), and chicken (in that order).
    4. Fill the pot up with 2 cups of chicken stock (or until it covers most of the mixture except the top).
    5. Place 4 curry blocks on the top. Make sure they are not touching the chicken stock.
    6. Switch the Instant-pot to Pressure-cook mode (high pressure) or stew/soup mode and cover with pressure lid for 15 minutes.
    7. After the Instant-pot is done, release the pressure and take off the lid.
    8. Mix the stew mixture together for 30 seconds or until the curry cubes are dissolved, then add the 1 tbs of soy sauce, 2 tbs of citrus (optional) and 1 tbs of ketchup.
    9. Lightly mix again and then switch the Instant-pot back to Saute mode for an additional 5 minutes.

  3. Enjoy your Japanese curry!

    1. Switch-off the Instant-pot (set to warm if needed).
    2. Your Japanese curry is now done! Please enjoy over rice and/or with katsu!
